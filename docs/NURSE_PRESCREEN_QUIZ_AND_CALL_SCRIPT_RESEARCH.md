# Nurse prescreen quiz / call script research and proposed question set

**Status:** research / product planning only. Not legal advice. Not a clinical protocol until reviewed and approved by the responsible Australian clinician(s).

**Scope:** PouchCare nurse prescreen after account signup and Step 1 patient prescreen quiz, before doctor review/consult.

---

## 1. What nurse prescreening should do

The nurse prescreen is not a prescribing consult. It should:

- confirm identity and privacy
- confirm the patient is reachable and able to participate in telehealth
- clarify incomplete or inconsistent intake answers
- confirm smoking/vaping/nicotine history
- assess nicotine dependence indicators
- check red flags and escalation needs
- check medicines/allergies/conditions relevant to nicotine cessation or nicotine products
- reinforce that prescription is not guaranteed
- prepare concise notes for the doctor
- refer/escalate where appropriate

The nurse must not:

- diagnose
- prescribe
- promise approval
- imply the script is ready
- override doctor review
- give product-specific directions beyond approved general education/scripts

## 3. Proposed nurse prescreen structure

### Section A — Opening, identity, privacy and consent

1. Confirm nurse identity and purpose:
   - “Hi, I’m [name], a nurse calling from PouchCare. This is a prescreen call before the doctor reviews your case.”

2. Confirm no guarantee:
   - “This call does not guarantee a prescription. The doctor makes the final clinical decision after consultation.”

3. Confirm identity:
   - full legal name
   - date of birth
   - residential address
   - current state/territory location
   - callback phone number

4. Confirm privacy/safety of call:
   - “Are you somewhere private and comfortable to discuss health information?”
   - “Is anyone else present?”
   - “Do you need an interpreter or support person?”
   - “Do you consent to continue and for me to document this call for the doctor?”

### Section B — Reason for seeking care

5. “Can you tell me in your own words what you’re hoping PouchCare can help with?”

6. “Are you mainly seeking help for smoking, vaping, nicotine dependence, switching away from another nicotine product, or something else?”

7. “What outcome are you hoping for?”
   - quit nicotine completely
   - stop smoking
   - stop vaping
   - reduce harm / transition away from another product
   - relapse prevention
   - other

### Section C — Tobacco, vaping and nicotine history

8. “Do you currently smoke tobacco?”
   - yes/no
   - cigarettes/day
   - years smoked
   - time to first cigarette after waking

9. “Do you currently vape?”
   - yes/no
   - nicotine/non-nicotine/unsure
   - frequency
   - nicotine strength if known
   - source: pharmacy / overseas / vape shop / friend / other

10. “Do you currently use nicotine pouches or other oral nicotine products?”
   - yes/no
   - brand/strength if known
   - pouches/day
   - how long used

11. “Do you use any other nicotine products?”
   - patches/gum/lozenges/spray/inhalator
   - heated tobacco
   - snus/chew
   - other

12. “Have you used tobacco-free nicotine pouches before?”
   - yes/no
   - what strength
   - what worked
   - side effects/problems

### Section D — Dependence / withdrawal / prior quit attempts

13. “How soon after waking do you usually use nicotine?”
   - within 5 min
   - 6–30 min
   - 31–60 min
   - over 60 min
   - not daily

14. “Do you get cravings or withdrawal symptoms if you do not use nicotine?”
   - irritability
   - anxiety/restlessness
   - low mood
   - poor concentration
   - sleep issues
   - increased appetite
   - headaches
   - other

15. “Have you tried to quit or reduce before?”
   - yes/no
   - how many attempts
   - longest quit period
   - what helped
   - what caused relapse

16. “Have you used any quit supports before?”
   - Quitline
   - GP/pharmacist support
   - counselling/behavioural support
   - nicotine patches/gum/lozenges/spray
   - varenicline
   - bupropion
   - vaping products
   - pouches
   - other

17. “Any side effects or bad experiences with quit medicines or nicotine products?”

### Section E — First-line therapy and behavioural support check

18. “Have you tried approved nicotine replacement therapy such as patches, gum, lozenges, spray or inhalator?”

19. “Have you discussed quitting with your GP, pharmacist or Quitline?”

20. “Would you like a Quitline referral or information about behavioural support?”

21. “Are you open to combining behavioural support with pharmacotherapy if the doctor recommends it?”

### Section F — Current medicines, allergies and interaction flags

22. “Can you confirm all current medicines, including prescription, over-the-counter, supplements and recreational substances?”

23. “Do you have any allergies or serious adverse reactions to medicines, nicotine products, flavours, excipients or adhesives?”

24. “Are you taking any medicines that may need review if you stop or reduce smoking?”
   Nurse should specifically flag if the patient reports:
   - clozapine
   - olanzapine
   - theophylline
   - warfarin or anticoagulants
   - insulin/diabetes medicines
   - caffeine-heavy use
   - antipsychotics/mental-health medicines
   - cancer/respiratory specialist medicines
   - heart-rhythm medicines such as flecainide

Reason: Quit Centre drug-interaction guidance says tobacco smoke, not nicotine, can affect metabolism of some medicines; stopping smoking can increase drug levels and require review.

### Section G — Medical and mental health safety screen

25. “Do you have any major medical conditions?”
   Prompt if needed:
   - heart disease
   - recent heart attack/stroke/TIA
   - unstable angina
   - severe arrhythmia
   - uncontrolled blood pressure
   - lung disease/asthma/COPD
   - seizures
   - kidney/liver disease
   - diabetes
   - cancer treatment

26. “Any current or past mental health conditions we should flag for the doctor?”
   - anxiety/depression
   - bipolar disorder
   - psychosis
   - eating disorder
   - substance-use disorder
   - self-harm/suicidal thoughts

27. “Any current thoughts of self-harm or suicide, or feeling unsafe today?”
   - If yes: urgent escalation pathway, not routine prescreen.

28. “Are you pregnant, breastfeeding, or trying to conceive?”
   - If yes: doctor priority review; do not progress as routine.

### Section H — Oral-health screening for pouches

Because nicotine pouches sit in the mouth, ask:

29. “Do you currently have mouth ulcers, gum disease, bleeding gums, oral infections, dental pain, or recent oral surgery?”

30. “Have nicotine pouches or oral products caused mouth irritation, gum irritation, nausea, hiccups, dizziness, or other side effects?”

31. “Do you have any history of oral cancer or significant oral lesions?”

Escalate to doctor/in-person dental/medical review if concerning symptoms are present.

### Section I — Product/request clarification

32. “What product type or strength were you hoping to discuss with the doctor?”

33. “How did you choose that product/strength?”

34. “How many pouches/products do you think you would use per day?”

35. “Do you understand the doctor may recommend a different option, lower amount, no prescription, or in-person review?”

36. “Do you understand the goal should be reducing harm and ideally reducing nicotine dependence over time, not increasing nicotine use?”

### Section J — Personal importation / fulfilment understanding

37. “Do you understand that if a prescription/written authority is issued, it is only because the doctor considers it clinically appropriate?”

38. “Do you understand that imported products are for your personal use only and must not be shared or resold?”

39. “Do you understand quantity limits may apply, including the 3-month supply per import and 15-month total in 12 months under the Personal Importation Scheme?”

40. “Do you understand imported products may be seized if they do not comply with requirements or do not match the prescription/written authority?”

### Section K — Final clarification and nurse outcome

41. “Has anything changed since you completed the online quiz?”

42. “Is there anything important the doctor should know that was not captured?”

43. “Do you have any questions for the doctor?”

44. Nurse selects outcome:
   - proceed to doctor review
   - proceed, but missing information required
   - priority doctor review due to clinical concern
   - recommend GP/in-person care before proceeding
   - urgent care advised
   - unable to verify identity
   - patient withdrew

## 4. Nurse red-flag escalation list

Escalate to doctor or urgent care pathway if:

- under 18 / age mismatch
- unable to verify identity
- patient not currently in Australia or state/territory unclear
- chest pain, severe shortness of breath, collapse/fainting, acute neurological symptoms
- active suicidal thoughts/self-harm risk
- pregnancy/breastfeeding/trying to conceive
- recent heart attack, stroke/TIA, unstable angina, severe arrhythmia
- severe uncontrolled mental illness or substance-use concern
- concerning medicine interaction risk, especially clozapine/olanzapine/theophylline/insulin/flecainide or specialist medicines
- current serious oral lesions, oral infection, unexplained mouth bleeding, oral cancer history
- adverse reaction to nicotine products
- signs of misuse, resale, product diversion, or someone else completing the process
- patient expects guaranteed approval or refuses doctor consultation

## 6. Recommended product rule

The nurse prescreen should produce a structured summary for the doctor but should never produce `approved`, `eligible_for_prescription`, or `script_ready`.

Suggested statuses:

- `nurse_prescreen_pending`
- `nurse_prescreen_completed_doctor_review_required`
- `missing_information_before_doctor_review`
- `priority_doctor_review_required`
- `in_person_or_gp_review_recommended`
- `urgent_care_recommended`
- `identity_unverified`
- `patient_withdrew`

Doctor review and real-time consult remain mandatory before prescription/written authority generation.
